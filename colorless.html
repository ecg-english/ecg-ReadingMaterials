ECG Reading Materials

Colorless

Fiction

Original Text ▼

To Ren, the world was not merely black and white; it was made of ash. The sky was a sheet of dirty lead, the trees were jagged scars of charcoal, and even the blood that flowed from a cut was the color of old oil. This color blindness was a curse that extended beyond sight—it muted his other senses. Strawberries tasted like wet paper. Music sounded like distant static. He was a painter starving for a sensation he could physically verify, trapped in a reality that felt like a decomposing film reel.


"You look like you're trying to eat the canvas," a voice dripped from the shadows.


The devil sat on his windowsill. She wore a tailored suit, but her fingers were too long, possessing too many joints. Her horns were not bone, but made of solidified black ink.


"I can give you the spectrum, Ren. But I don't want your soul. Souls are boring," she sneered, her tongue flicking out—long, purple, and bifurcated. "I want the raw material. Art is just emotion frozen in time, isn't it? I am a collector of... authentic pigments."


"Pigments?" Ren asked, his voice raspy from disuse.


"Your memories. Specifically, the ones that make you human. Fear makes a beautiful Cobalt Blue. Shame is the perfect Ochre. I digest them, refine them, and vomit them back out as pure Color. You get your masterpiece; I get a meal. Deal?"


Ren didn't hesitate. The hunger for color was a madness that clawed at his throat. "Take it. Take the night my father broke my arm."


The devil didn't use magic. She straddled him, forcing his jaw open with her multi-jointed fingers. She reached deep into his throat, past his gag reflex, her claws scraping his esophagus. Ren convulsed, tears of pain streaming down his face, as she slowly pulled out a shimmering, viscous string of gray slime. It was the physical manifestation of his trauma. She swallowed it whole, her throat bulging obscenely.


Then, she spat on his palette. A glob of violent, screaming Red.


Ren painted like a man possessed. The Red was visceral; it smelled of iron and rage.


But one color wasn't enough. The gray returned, hungrier than before.


Ren sold his shame for Yellow. He sold the memory of his mother's voice for Orange.


As his canvas became vibrant, his humanity hollowed out. He stopped calling friends because he sold the concept of "friendship" for a deep Indigo. He stopped feeling guilt because he traded his "conscience" for a brilliant Violet. He was becoming a monster, a hollow vessel that existed only to hold the brush.


Finally, he needed Green. The color of life. The color of the woman he used to love.


He looked at the photo of his late wife. He knew he should feel hesitation. He knew that forgetting her was a blasphemy. But looking at the photo, he felt nothing but a calculation of hue and saturation.


"The grief of her death," Ren whispered to the devil. "It's heavy. It must be worth a lot."


"Oh, it is," the devil purred, drooling slightly. "Grief is the most complex flavor. It's bitter, sweet, and eternal. It will make the most beautiful Green this world has ever seen."


She pried his mouth open again. This time, the extraction took hours. It was a spiritual vivisection. She dragged the heavy, wet weight of his mourning out of his soul.


The Green she produced was divine. It pulsed with a heartbeat.


At the gallery opening, the world wept. Critics fell to their knees before the painting titled "The Garden." It depicted a woman standing in a field of grass so vivid it seemed to produce oxygen.


"It's a miracle," a journalist gasped, wiping her eyes. She turned to Ren, who stood sipping champagne, his eyes scanning the crowd with the cold detachment of a reptile. "Sir, the emotion in this green... it's overwhelming. You must have loved this model deeply. Do you miss her?"


Ren looked at the painting.


"Why would I miss a tube of paint?"

和訳 ▼

To Ren, the world was not merely black and white; it was made of ash. The sky was a sheet of dirty lead, the trees were jagged scars of charcoal, and even the blood that flowed from a cut was the color of old oil.

レンにとって、世界は単に白黒ではなかった；それは灰でできていた。空は汚れた鉛のシートであり、木々は炭のギザギザした傷跡であり、切り傷から流れる血でさえ古い油の色だった。

This color blindness was a curse that extended beyond sight—it muted his other senses. Strawberries tasted like wet paper. Music sounded like distant static.

この色覚異常は視覚を超えて広がる呪いだった——それは彼の他の感覚を鈍らせた。イチゴは濡れた紙のような味がした。音楽は遠くの雑音のように聞こえた。

He was a painter starving for a sensation he could physically verify, trapped in a reality that felt like a decomposing film reel.

彼は物理的に確認できる感覚に飢えた画家であり、腐敗するフィルムリールのように感じられる現実に閉じ込められていた。

"You look like you're trying to eat the canvas," a voice dripped from the shadows.

「キャンバスを食べようとしているように見えますね」影から声が滴り落ちた。

The devil sat on his windowsill. She wore a tailored suit, but her fingers were too long, possessing too many joints. Her horns were not bone, but made of solidified black ink.

悪魔が彼の窓枠に座っていた。彼女は仕立てのスーツを着ていたが、指が長すぎ、関節が多すぎた。彼女の角は骨ではなく、固まった黒いインクでできていた。

"I can give you the spectrum, Ren. But I don't want your soul. Souls are boring," she sneered, her tongue flicking out—long, purple, and bifurcated.

「レン、私はあなたにスペクトルを与えることができます。しかし、あなたの魂は欲しくありません。魂は退屈です」彼女は嘲笑し、舌を出した——長く、紫色で、二股に分かれていた。

"I want the raw material. Art is just emotion frozen in time, isn't it? I am a collector of... authentic pigments."

「私は原材料が欲しいのです。芸術は時間に凍りついた感情に過ぎない、そうでしょう？私は...本物の顔料の収集家です」

"Pigments?" Ren asked, his voice raspy from disuse.

「顔料？」レンは尋ねた。彼の声は使われていないためにかすれていた。

"Your memories. Specifically, the ones that make you human. Fear makes a beautiful Cobalt Blue. Shame is the perfect Ochre. I digest them, refine them, and vomit them back out as pure Color. You get your masterpiece; I get a meal. Deal?"

「あなたの記憶です。具体的には、あなたを人間にするもの。恐怖は美しいコバルトブルーを作ります。恥は完璧なオーカーです。私はそれらを消化し、精製し、純粋な色として吐き戻します。あなたは傑作を得る；私は食事を得る。取引ですか？」

Ren didn't hesitate. The hunger for color was a madness that clawed at his throat. "Take it. Take the night my father broke my arm."

レンはためらわなかった。色への飢えは彼の喉を掻きむしる狂気だった。「取ってくれ。父が私の腕を折った夜を取ってくれ」

The devil didn't use magic. She straddled him, forcing his jaw open with her multi-jointed fingers. She reached deep into his throat, past his gag reflex, her claws scraping his esophagus.

悪魔は魔法を使わなかった。彼女は彼にまたがり、多関節の指で彼の顎を無理やり開けた。彼女は彼の喉の奥深くに手を伸ばし、嘔吐反射を越えて、爪で彼の食道を引っかいた。

Ren convulsed, tears of pain streaming down his face, as she slowly pulled out a shimmering, viscous string of gray slime. It was the physical manifestation of his trauma. She swallowed it whole, her throat bulging obscenely.

レンは痙攣し、痛みの涙が顔を流れ落ちた。彼女がきらめく、粘性のある灰色の粘液の糸をゆっくりと引き出したとき。それは彼のトラウマの物理的な現れだった。彼女はそれを丸ごと飲み込み、喉が不気味に膨らんだ。

Then, she spat on his palette. A glob of violent, screaming Red.

それから、彼女は彼のパレットに唾を吐いた。暴力的で、叫ぶような赤の塊。

Ren painted like a man possessed. The Red was visceral; it smelled of iron and rage.

レンは憑かれた男のように描いた。赤は内臓的だった；鉄と怒りの匂いがした。

But one color wasn't enough. The gray returned, hungrier than before.

しかし、一つの色では十分ではなかった。灰色が戻ってきた。以前よりも飢えていた。

Ren sold his shame for Yellow. He sold the memory of his mother's voice for Orange.

レンは恥を黄色と交換した。彼は母親の声の記憶をオレンジと交換した。

As his canvas became vibrant, his humanity hollowed out. He stopped calling friends because he sold the concept of "friendship" for a deep Indigo. He stopped feeling guilt because he traded his "conscience" for a brilliant Violet.

キャンバスが鮮やかになるにつれて、彼の人間性は空洞化した。彼は「友情」の概念を深いインディゴと交換したため、友人に電話するのをやめた。彼は「良心」を輝くバイオレットと交換したため、罪悪感を感じるのをやめた。

He was becoming a monster, a hollow vessel that existed only to hold the brush.

彼は怪物になりつつあった。筆を持つためだけに存在する空洞の器。

Finally, he needed Green. The color of life. The color of the woman he used to love.

ついに、彼は緑が必要だった。生命の色。かつて愛した女性の色。

He looked at the photo of his late wife. He knew he should feel hesitation. He knew that forgetting her was a blasphemy. But looking at the photo, he felt nothing but a calculation of hue and saturation.

彼は亡き妻の写真を見た。ためらいを感じるべきだと知っていた。彼女を忘れることは冒涜だと知っていた。しかし、写真を見て、彼は色相と彩度の計算以外何も感じなかった。

"The grief of her death," Ren whispered to the devil. "It's heavy. It must be worth a lot."

「彼女の死の悲しみ」レンは悪魔にささやいた。「それは重い。きっと価値があるはずだ」

"Oh, it is," the devil purred, drooling slightly. "Grief is the most complex flavor. It's bitter, sweet, and eternal. It will make the most beautiful Green this world has ever seen."

「ああ、そうです」悪魔は低く唸り、少しよだれを垂らした。「悲しみは最も複雑な味です。苦く、甘く、永遠です。それはこの世界がこれまで見た中で最も美しい緑を作るでしょう」

She pried his mouth open again. This time, the extraction took hours. It was a spiritual vivisection. She dragged the heavy, wet weight of his mourning out of his soul.

彼女は再び彼の口をこじ開けた。今回は、抽出に数時間かかった。それは精神的な生体解剖だった。彼女は彼の魂から、重く、湿った悲しみの重みを引きずり出した。

The Green she produced was divine. It pulsed with a heartbeat.

彼女が作り出した緑は神聖だった。それは心拍と共に脈打っていた。

At the gallery opening, the world wept. Critics fell to their knees before the painting titled "The Garden." It depicted a woman standing in a field of grass so vivid it seemed to produce oxygen.

ギャラリーのオープニングで、世界は泣いた。批評家たちは「庭」というタイトルの絵の前にひざまずいた。それは、酸素を生み出しているように見えるほど鮮やかな草の野原に立つ女性を描いていた。

"It's a miracle," a journalist gasped, wiping her eyes. She turned to Ren, who stood sipping champagne, his eyes scanning the crowd with the cold detachment of a reptile.

「それは奇跡です」ジャーナリストは息を切らし、目を拭いた。彼女はレンに向き直った。レンはシャンパンをすすりながら立ち、爬虫類のような冷たい無関心で群衆をスキャンしていた。

"Sir, the emotion in this green... it's overwhelming. You must have loved this model deeply. Do you miss her?"

「先生、この緑の感情...圧倒的です。あなたはこのモデルを深く愛していたに違いありません。彼女を恋しく思いますか？」

Ren looked at the painting.

レンは絵を見た。

"Why would I miss a tube of paint?"

「なぜ絵の具のチューブを恋しく思う必要があるのか？」

文法解説 ▼

not merely（単に〜ではない）

"the world was not merely black and white"

"not merely" は「単に〜ではない」という意味で、より深い内容を強調します。

ここでは「世界は単に白黒ではなかった」という意味です。

例：It's not merely a problem; it's a crisis. (それは単なる問題ではない；それは危機だ)

made of（〜でできた）

"it was made of ash"

"made of" は「〜でできた」という意味で、材料を表します。

ここでは「それは灰でできていた」という意味です。

例：The table is made of wood. (テーブルは木でできている)

関係代名詞 that（制限用法）

"a curse that extended beyond sight"

関係代名詞 that は「視覚を超えて広がる呪い」という意味です。

ここでは、呪いの範囲を説明しています。

例：a problem that affects everyone (すべての人に影響を与える問題)

sound like（〜のように聞こえる）

"Music sounded like distant static"

"sound like" は「〜のように聞こえる」という意味です。

ここでは「音楽は遠くの雑音のように聞こえた」という意味です。

例：It sounds like rain. (雨のように聞こえる)

starving for（〜に飢えている）

"a painter starving for a sensation"

"starving for" は「〜に飢えている」という意味で、強い欲求を表します。

ここでは「感覚に飢えた画家」という意味です。

例：I'm starving for knowledge. (私は知識に飢えている)

過去分詞句（後置修飾）

"trapped in a reality that felt like a decomposing film reel"

過去分詞句は名詞を後ろから修飾し、「〜された」という意味を表します。

ここでは「腐敗するフィルムリールのように感じられる現実に閉じ込められた」という意味です。

例：a bird trapped in a cage (檻に閉じ込められた鳥)

look like（〜のように見える）

"You look like you're trying to eat the canvas"

"look like" は「〜のように見える」という意味です。

ここでは「キャンバスを食べようとしているように見える」という意味です。

例：It looks like it's going to rain. (雨が降りそうに見える)

現在分詞句（後置修飾）

"her fingers were too long, possessing too many joints"

現在分詞句は名詞を後ろから修飾し、「〜している」という意味を表します。

ここでは「関節が多すぎる指を持っている」という意味です。

例：a person possessing great talent (才能を持っている人)

I don't want（〜は欲しくない）

"I don't want your soul"

"I don't want" は「〜は欲しくない」という意味で、否定の願望を表します。

ここでは「あなたの魂は欲しくない」という意味です。

例：I don't want trouble. (トラブルは欲しくない)

付加疑問文（isn't it?）

"Art is just emotion frozen in time, isn't it?"

付加疑問文は、文の後に「〜ですよね？」と確認を求める表現です。

ここでは「芸術は時間に凍りついた感情に過ぎない、そうでしょう？」という意味です。

例：It's a nice day, isn't it? (いい天気ですね、そうでしょう？)

否定形（didn't + 動詞の原形）
